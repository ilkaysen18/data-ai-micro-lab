Project Overview

Title: Humans and the Shrinking Job Market: Student Futures and Human Resilience in Türkiye’s AI Economy

This project explores how the accelerating automation and AI-driven restructuring of work in Türkiye will shape the career hopes, economic security, and emotional resilience of students and young professionals. It aims to generate insights and practical recommendations that preserve human dignity and economic stability in the face of technological disruption.

Rationale

As AI-driven automation transforms industries, Türkiye’s youth — particularly university students and early graduates — face a new dilemma: they are digitally literate yet economically vulnerable. Many are being educated for jobs that may soon vanish or change dramatically. The project focuses on mapping these risks and identifying resilience strategies to ensure a humane and equitable transition to the AI economy.

Focus Areas

  • Perceptions of the Future: How do students feel about employability in an AI-dominated job market?
  • Language, Education, and Access: How does English proficiency and access to global resources affect readiness?
  • Human-Centered Resilience: Which human traits (empathy, creativity, adaptability) remain essential?
  • Equity and Systemic Risk: Who is most at risk of displacement and impoverishment, and how do we design protective systems?

Methodology (6‑Month Pilot)

Phase Activity Output
1. Insights & Hypothesis
(Month 1)		 Literature scan; define qualitative and quantitative hypotheses around student concerns. 1–2 page hypothesis brief
2. Student Survey
(Month 2–3)		 Run bilingual survey: "AI and My Future Career: Youth Voices in Türkiye". Measure optimism, fear, readiness. Survey dataset + descriptive analysis
3. Focus Groups
(Month 3–4)		 Group interviews on AI awareness, career anxiety, and adaptive strategies. Human-stories transcripts + empathy maps
4. Analysis & Synthesis
(Month 5)		 Correlate qualitative and quantitative results (e.g., optimism vs. field of study). Policy-relevant insight brief
5. Reporting
(Month 6)		 Publish bilingual report and create accessible online summary. “Student Futures in an Automated Türkiye” white paper

Expected Deliverables

  • Open-Access Report & Dataset: Bilingual publication on youth perspectives and resilience strategies.
  • Human Impact Framework: A model for understanding vulnerability and adaptability amid automation.
  • Educational/Policy Recommendations: Guides for universities to foster ethics, adaptability, and AI literacy.
  • Seed Funding Proposal: Future TÜBİTAK / EU Horizon submission on human-centered reskilling.

Guiding Philosophy

“Technology may reshape tasks, but humanity must redefine value.
We aim to ensure that students in Türkiye — and across societies — are equipped not just to survive the AI transition, but to stay human while doing it.”
